introduction_text: |
  Hannah Rickards's practice studies the relationship between perception and experience. She uses a range of conceptual tools and media to plot the role of uncertainty in our attempts to discern and describe our environment. While her projects have often analyzed natural phenomena—for example, dissecting the sound of a thunderclap or plotting a community's disparate testimonies of a witnessed mirage—they have been less focused on the phenomena themselves than on generating a set of frameworks for understanding them.

  I am the infant and I am the bird marks a subtle but significant shift in Rickards' focus, occasioned in part by her move to Canada from the UK in 2019 and then to an acreage in Syilx Okanagan territory in the interior of British Columbia in 2022. Life in this new context, with its markedly different tempo and scent—a rural valley thick with orchards, pasture, and lumber mills, encircled by rocky benchland—invoked a kind of metabolic shift in the artist's approach. While the question of where and how we place our attention has always been fundamental to Rickards's work, I am the infant and I am the bird asks its viewers to uncouple themselves from contemporary culture's relentless speed of image consumption to allow for a quiet alertness to the world's vitality.

  I am the infant and I am the bird also shares much with early interpretations of photography. For the first decade of its existence, the photographic image was understood not as "captured" or "taken" but rather as something received from the world. Photographers were obliged to wait patiently for their images to appear and often spoke of an inability (both chemically and psychically) to fully control or fix the medium. Rickards' work, too, is about the potency of waiting. As she meditates on questions of distance, place, and temporality (whether considering the movements of hummingbirds or of geological formations), she invites a consideration of how we might more carefully attend to the world's ways of revealing itself to us.
